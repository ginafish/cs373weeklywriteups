Forensics Basics

Key things: can you replicate it? Can you explain it? Yes you can analyze memory dumps! It's a core step. Only thing you are proving is what happened on the system.

When do you do invesigations? Fraud, Intellectual Property Theft, Hacker Intrusions/Data Breaches, Inappropriate use of network, Child exploitation, eDiscovery Supporting (Civil and Criminal Litigation)

Forensics Steps: Evidence acquisition, Investigation and analysis, Reporting results Three categories: Life forensics, Post-mortem based forensics (memory/disk), Network based forensics Can use md5 checksums to compare to databases to check if child porn Have to learn how to explain to judges in a way they understand Forensics Nutshell: identify evidence, preserve evidence, analyze evidence, present results Four principles: minimize data loss, record everything, analyze all data collected, report findings May need to present to a court of law First thing you always do: record your time and the system time Might be on a corporate time sync server, and be 5+ minutes off Difficult things that might be useful: car gps!
